Part 1: Competitive Analysis

First off, what is competitive analysis? Competitive analysis is the process of researching competitors to gain insight on their own performance. In this project, I conducted competitive analysis on three different websites containing a similar favoriting function. The theme that I am exploring is clothing stores across the three websites. This is because I like to do retail therapy whenever I am stressed by scrolling through different clothing websites, but due to my lack of commitment, I rarely buy anything. Summary

Summary: After studying the three websites, I reached the following conclusions:

  • Most Successful: The most successful feature was being able to filter different clothing sizes on the main webpage.
  • Justification: Since all of the websites are clothing apparel companies, this feature was expected from me as all three websites had this functionality. The main design principle here is to maximize usability in the interface where usability is the attribute that looks at how easy it is for the user to use an interface. Since it is the expectation of the user to have a filtering option, to increase usability, a lot of websites would have this functionality which is the justification as to why it is the most successful feature on the chart.

  • Least Successful: The least successful feature was being able to place all of the favorited items (using the favoriting function) into the shopping cart aggregator for checkout.
  • Justification: This was surprising as none of the websites had this functionality, but this was one of the features that I thought would be convenient to have inside of the favoriting function interface. The justification as to why this might be is that companies did not feel that this feature was a high enough priority. This leads us back to the in-class activity that we did on 3/12, where we were asked to pick which features that we thought were most important to complete in a current sprint with a limited amount of hours to complete such features. Therefore, the design practice that might explain this is that designers for these websites prioritized other features, leaving this feature of the chart on the chopping block.

List of Features

List of Features: Based off my competitive analysis, the list of features that I will attempt to implement in my React application will be the following:

  • Ability to filter different clothing items
  • Ability to favorite a clothing item
  • Ability to place a clothing item in a shopping cart
  • Ability to sort items by price
  • Ability to filter items by price
  • Ability to filter items by whether they are a top or a bottom piece
  • Reset filter/sorting functionality

One clarification that I wanted to make is that the favorited list and the shopping cart are separate. There could be items that are favorited inside of the shopping cart, but there could also be items that are not favorited that are inside of the shopping cart as well. These two lists are kept separate to help the user finalize their decisions.

Part 2: React Application

Planning

Now that we have conducted our competitive analysis, we need to start planning out our interface.


Planning: Based off my competitive analysis in the seciton above, I want to create my own version of a clothing apparel site that incorporates all of the features that are listed inside of the "List of Features" section above. Additionally, this will be the ways that I incorporate filter, sort, and aggregation below:
  • Filter: For filtering, you will be able to filter based off certain criteria which will include filtering for just top pieces (i.e., shirts, sweatshirts) and for bottoms(i.e., pants, shorts). Additionally, we can filter based off of price!
  • Sort: For sorting, you will be able to sort based off price, so you will be able to sort via the highest price and the lowest price as well.
  • Aggregation: For aggregation, there will be an option on each clothing item to favorite the clothing item. Additionally, there will be an option to add an item to a shopping cart where a subtotal will be calculated!

Clarifications: Here are some clarifications of the application below:
  • First, to favorite an item, you click on the heart icon on the specific item card. This change will be reflected in the favorites aggregator.

To filter tops and then filter bottoms, you need to reset in between to go back to an unfiltered state.
